Anchorage 1770

Category

Branding | Web Design

Date

Winter 2014

  • Share it:

Visit Site

Challenge

To develop a website and logo for a client that was renovating an historic landmark. Anchorage 1770 is a Botique B&B who also needed booking widgets, google map implementation and gift certificate shopping cart.

Solution

For an establishment as old and historied as The Anchorage it was essential to stay true to it's roots while not being lost in the past. The overall direction of the project was focused the locality of Beaufort and the property's history.

Result

The overall success was reached through implementation of some very exciting customized content-managed mapping and the creation of a new Identity. The "Anchor" mark is a more than well known icon and carries great meaning in many circles. I believe that it is successful in not being overused while remaining classical and elegant.

The Maine String

Category

Logo Design | Packaging

Date

Fall 2013

  • Share it:

Challenge

Home-made Etsy-ish Knitware shop needed new logo and packaging. Something fresh and modern, while remaining true to what Mainers are.

Solution

It's far too easy to directly enumerate a part of a clients brand name. So the effort was to do all we could to not use the state as part of the logo. We also wanted to create a feeling of an eco-friendly company whose product and packaging could be handmade.

Result

By distilling iconography from the knitting process into very flat graphics and utilizing a subtle color scheme, we came upon a final product that would be dynamic to future product types and was easy enough to print at home in various manners.

Platinum ABQ

Category

Web Design | Advertising

Site Launce

January 2015

  • Share it:

Visit Site

Challenge

The Client had created a very modern luxury apartment complex in the heart of Albequrques hippest district, Nob Hill. Their digital and market presence needed to relfect the standard of living they represented. The main challenge was to create an a site that would allow potential residents to shop and apply for apartments of their choosing.

Solution

The build decided on was a web app that would allow the user to interact with the building and choose which unit best matched their needs.

Result

The site is a very linear story. A user traverses through what a day for a Platinum resident would be, then is presented with their options for which apartments suit them, and finally an application.

Stunning. I love the mountains and sunset in Abq and Platinum provides a mesmerizing view. You capture the best asset of all.
